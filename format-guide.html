World Cup 2026 Format

Everything you need to know about the new 48-team format

Overview: FIFA World Cup 2026 will be the largest World Cup ever, featuring 48 teams and 104 matches across the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

1. Tournament Structure

The FIFA World Cup 2026 will feature 48 national teams divided into 12 groups of 4 teams each.

  • 48 Teams
  • 12 Groups
  • 4 Teams per Group
  • 104 Matches Total
2. Group Stage

Every team plays three group-stage matches. Teams earn:

  • 3 points for a win
  • 1 point for a draw
  • 0 points for a loss

The top two teams from each group automatically qualify for the knockout stage.

3. Best Third-Placed Teams

In addition to the 24 teams finishing first or second, the 8 best third-placed teams also qualify.

This creates a 32-team knockout bracket.

4. Knockout Stage
  • Round of 32
  • Round of 16
  • Quarter-finals
  • Semi-finals
  • Third-place Match
  • Final

Every knockout match must produce a winner. If scores are level after 90 minutes, extra time and penalties may be used.

5. Tie-Breakers

If teams finish level on points, FIFA uses tie-breakers including:

  • Goal Difference
  • Goals Scored
  • Head-to-Head Results
  • Fair Play Record
  • Drawing of Lots
6. Hosts

FIFA World Cup 2026 will be jointly hosted by:

  • United States
  • Canada
  • Mexico

It will be the first World Cup hosted by three nations.
