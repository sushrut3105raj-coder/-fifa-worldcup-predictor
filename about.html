Background

The Story

The FIFA World Cup 2026 is the biggest football tournament ever held — 48 nations, 104 matches, three host countries, and over a month of football. We wanted a prediction tool that actually matched the scale of the event.

Most prediction tools ask you to manually pick winners. We thought: why not let the scores decide everything? Enter a scoreline, and the table updates. Fill in all six group games and the top two teams advance automatically. Best eight third-placed teams are ranked globally. The bracket fills itself.

The result is a simulation platform that feels less like clicking buttons and more like genuinely running the tournament from your browser — no sign-up, no cost, no friction.

We've built the correct FIFA 2026 qualification engine from scratch, including the official third-place global ranking tiebreakers and the proper Round of 32 bracket allocation so no group-stage opponents can meet before the semi-finals.
